My Story

Building real skills before the world expects me to.

I'm a 15-year-old high school student in Marshfield, Massachusetts, and I built this research platform from scratch — not for a class, not for a grade, but because I'm genuinely obsessed with how markets and businesses work.

What started as reading earnings reports and following the Fed turned into weekly long-form analysis, daily market briefings, and a free custom research service for businesses and founders. I do this outside of school, on my own time, because I believe the best way to learn is to actually do the work.

I compete in DECA — a business and marketing competition where I apply real strategy frameworks to case studies — and I race in alpine ski racing, where I've learned that preparation, discipline, and execution under pressure are what separate good from great. I play lacrosse too, and team sports have shaped how I think about collaboration and accountability.

My goal right now is simple: build a track record of real, high-quality work so that when real opportunities come — internships, mentorships, partnerships — I've already earned them.

I'm not waiting until I'm 22 to start taking business seriously. Every report I publish, every analysis I deliver — that's real experience. That's my portfolio.
— Nolan Cochran
